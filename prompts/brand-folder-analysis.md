## Primary Logo Analysis

**Visual composition of the logo:**
- **Symbol:** An inverted equilateral triangle (pointing downward) with a hand-drawn, textured stroke — not a digital clean line. The stroke has slight ink-bleed quality, making it feel crafted rather than machined.
- **Wordmark:** "TrailVolt" in a bold, modern sans-serif. The "T", "r", "a", "i", "l" are in deep navy (#0D1B2A approximately), and "V", "o", "l", "t" are in a slightly lighter grey-blue — creating a subtle two-tone effect within a single word.
- **Tagline:** "ONE PACK. EVERY HORIZON." in a clean, widely-tracked uppercase sans-serif, in medium grey.
- **Background:** The logo is shown on a very light grey/off-white background (#F5F5F5 approximately).
- **Overall format:** Horizontal lockup — symbol left, wordmark + tagline right.

**Symbol analysis:**
The inverted triangle is meaningful in the TrailVolt context:
- It suggests a downward direction — a trail descending, a mountain valley, a V-shape that forms the "V" in "Volt"
- The hand-drawn quality suggests craft, authenticity, human-made — not corporate
- The open interior (hollow triangle, not filled) suggests lightness and efficiency — not heavy gear, but purposeful design
- The slight imperfection in the stroke is intentional — it communicates the "Built out of frustration by a real person" brand story

## How the Logo Influences Image Generation

### What the logo tells us about visual tone:

**1. Craft over polish**
The hand-drawn symbol says: this brand values authenticity over corporate slickness. Product photography should feel real and earned — not over-retouched or artificially perfect. Materials should look like they have character, not like renders.

**2. Geometric structure**
The clean triangle geometry alongside the hand-drawn quality suggests a brand that combines rigour (the precise system logic of the modular design) with craft (the tactile, human-made quality of the product). Images should balance clean composition with real texture.

**3. Navy as brand base**
The deep navy in the wordmark is the same dark navy (#0B1626) used as the studio background. The logo and the product photographs share the same colour universe. This is intentional — the logo and the product photography are from the same brand world.

**4. Electric mint as contrast**
The logo does not use mint — but the brand's CSS and visual-foundation.md use mint as the primary accent. The logo reserves mint for the product world (the moment a module snaps, the backlight in studio shots). Mint in photography should feel like it belongs to the product, not to the brand identity design system.

**5. The tagline as image brief**
"ONE PACK. EVERY HORIZON." is the brief for every lifestyle image. Each lifestyle shot must visually communicate: one pack, a different horizon. Wicklow trails (Trail horizon), Dublin city (Everyday horizon), airport departure gate (Travel horizon).

## Premium Cues From the Logo

- **No icon alone** — the symbol always appears with the wordmark. This is a mark that earns its place through context, not through logo-icon dominance.
- **No bright accent in the mark** — the logo trusts the product to provide colour.
- **Tracking on tagline** — the wide letter-spacing on "ONE PACK. EVERY HORIZON." signals a brand with space, confidence, and premium positioning.
- **Proportional restraint** — the symbol and wordmark are balanced, neither element overwhelming the other.

## Summary: What the Brand Folder Tells Us

The TrailVolt brand is built on:
1. **A meaningful symbol** — the inverted triangle is not decorative; it's conceptually loaded with direction, craft, and the V in Volt
2. **Navy as the universe** — the brand's colour world starts from dark navy; everything else is in contrast to that
3. **Craft over corporate** — the hand-drawn mark sets a tone that the photography must match: real, earned, human
4. **Restraint** — no mint in the logo, no bright colours in the mark, no over-designed lockup — the product does the talking

For image generation, this means:
- Studio backgrounds must be exactly dark navy — not black, not charcoal, not dark grey
- The mint glow must feel like it belongs to the product itself, not to the brand design system
- Lifestyle shots must feel like real photographs, not brand shoots
- The pack must look expensive through material quality and hardware precision, not through styling or staging
