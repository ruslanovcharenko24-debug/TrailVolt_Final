## Brand Essence

**One sentence:** One pack system — built from real frustration — that eliminates the need for every other bag.

**Tagline:** One Pack. Every Horizon.

**Origin story:** Ruslan (the founder) owned four bags: a laptop backpack, a weekend duffel, a trail pack, a gym bag. Constant repacking. Forgotten chargers. Wrong shoes. He watched a man at Dublin Airport unclip a secondary bag from his main pack. That moment became TrailVolt. The name: "Trail" (every journey is one) + "Volt" (the energy the right gear gives you).

**Designed in Dublin. Made for everywhere.**

## Visual World

TrailVolt images exist in three visual environments:

### Environment 1 — The Studio (Product Photography)
Dark navy (`#0B1626`) seamless background. Cinematic rim lighting. Shallow depth of field. The pack at 3/4 angle, electric mint glow from behind. This is the flagship product world — think luxury watch commercial, not outdoor gear catalogue.

### Environment 2 — The City (Everyday / Travel Lifestyle)
Dublin, London, Barcelona, Amsterdam. Morning light. Commuters in motion. Real urban environments — cafés, cycling lanes, Liffey boardwalk, airport departure gates. The pack is visible but the person is in motion and intentional. Not posed. Not a corporate photoshoot.

### Environment 3 — The Trail (Outdoor / Trail Lifestyle)
Irish and Scottish highlands. Wild Atlantic Way. Pyrenees. Misty green hills, dramatic skies, golden hour. The pack is a working tool here — straps taut, compression loaded, weatherproof. No fake "nature" studio shots.

### Key Visual Logic
- The pack is always the **hero**, even in lifestyle shots — it's never incidental
- The person is **in motion or at work** — never posing for the camera
- The environment reinforces the **use case** of that line (Everyday = city, Trail = terrain, Travel = transit)
- Every image should feel like it was **taken on a real day** by someone who was actually there

## Material Cues (visible in the photography)

These physical details must read clearly in product shots — they are proof of premium construction:

- **Cordura 500D weave pattern** — visible textile grain, military-grade
- **YKK zipper pulls** — precise, matte finish, never plastic-looking
- **Duraflex buckle geometry** — load-rated, architectural-looking hardware
- **Module connection points** — the front-face attachment rail (stainless + nylon) should be visible as a design feature, not hidden
- **Back panel foam channels** — visible through the suspension system in back-facing shots
- **DWR coating surface** — water beads off the shell; this effect is powerful in Trail / Travel shots
- **Interior lining** — bright 210D nylon (easy-clean white/light interior shows quality)
- **Magnetic snap mechanism** — the moment a module connects is a key visual narrative

## Image Style Logic

### Studio product shots (`hero-pack-main.webp`, `product-*.webp`)
- Dark navy background
- 3/4 angle showing front face + one side
- Rim-lit from upper-left (sharp fabric definition)
- Soft electric mint backlight (glow, not halo)
- Ultra-sharp — fabric texture, zipper hardware must be legible
- No people, no hands

### Line card lifestyle images (`line-card-*.webp`)
- Real-world environment matching the line's use case
- Subject in motion — walking, hiking, cycling; never posed
- Natural or golden-hour light
- Pack clearly visible but subject is the story
- Cinematic crop — wide enough to establish the environment

### Shop product cards (`pack-*.webp`)
- Clean studio or semi-studio: neutral navy or gradient background
- Slightly elevated angle (looking down 15-20 degrees)
- Full front face of pack visible, all compartments shown
- Correct colourway — Core Black is dark matte, Slate Grey is lighter grey-toned, Forest Green is deep forest
- Lighting shows the specific texture of each shell material

### Module cards (`module-*.webp`)
- Product flat-lay on dark navy background
- Module isolated, well-lit, all features visible
- Mint accent light catches the connection hardware

### Tribe member portraits (`tribe-*.webp`)
- Location-specific lifestyle portrait
- Person is clearly active and in context (trail, café, cycling, airport, workshop)
- Pack is visible and in use
- Not a headshot — this is editorial photography

### About/founder images (`about-*.webp`)
- Authentic-feeling — workshop, Dublin, prototype stages
- Ruslan is the founder; images should feel intimate, not corporate
- Workshop: materials laid out, stitching in progress, early prototypes
- Wicklow Mountains: real Irish highland scenery, testing environment
- Airport: Dublin Airport, Travel pack in use

### Sustainability (`sustainability-repair.webp`)
- Spare parts laid out on dark surface
- YKK zipper pull, Duraflex buckle, thread spool, module connector
- Mint glow as each part arrives (meditative assembly)
- No waste, no landfill — the opposite of disposable

### OG images (`og-*.jpg`)
- 1200×630 horizontal format
- Pack prominently featured
- Brand name legible
- Consistent dark navy palette with mint accent

## What Should NEVER Appear in Generated Visuals

- Generic stock photos of anonymous lifestyle people
- Bright artificial colours that are not Core Black, Slate Grey, Forest Green, Electric Mint, or Navy
- Cheap-looking hardware — plastic clips, bright chrome zippers
- Gear that doesn't look modular (the front attachment face is the product's identity)
- Fake smiles, posed corporate body language
- White studio backgrounds (with exception: some module close-ups only)
- Backpacks that look like generic hiking or travel gear with no modular system
- Children, pets, vehicles (unless specifically contextual for travel line)
- Watermarks, logos, text overlaid on product photography
- Blurry, low-resolution, noisy, washed-out imagery
- AI "tell" artefacts: extra fingers, distorted zippers, melting hardware, impossible reflections

## What Makes a TrailVolt Image Feel "On-Brand"

1. **It looks like it was earned** — the shot exists because someone actually made a journey
2. **The pack looks expensive** — not through filters, but through material texture and hardware clarity
3. **The mint is a whisper, not a shout** — electric accent, not neon noise
4. **The environment is real** — Dublin rain, Wicklow mist, Barcelona light, Amsterdam canals
5. **The modularity is visible** — the front attachment face, a module in-situ, or the snap connection moment
6. **The scale is human** — 22L or 28L looks right on a real person's back, not massive, not tiny
7. **The person is at their best version of themselves** — capable, focused, in motion. Never stressed or cluttered.
8. **It could appear in Monocle or Gear Patrol** — not Pinterest mood board, not Amazon listing
