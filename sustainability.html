Built Responsibly

Less. Better. Longer.

The most sustainable pack is the one you never need to replace. TrailVolt is designed to last decades — and when something wears out, we fix it.

Our Commitments

Not aspirations. Not marketing language. These are the actual design and business decisions we make every day.

♻️

Recyclable Packaging

Every TrailVolt pack ships in 100% recyclable outer packaging. No virgin plastic. No polystyrene. Cardboard inner structure. Tissue paper. A single-use plastic-free unboxing experience that still feels premium.

🔧

Designed for Repair

Repairability is an intentional design parameter — not an afterthought. Every component that is likely to wear — zippers, buckles, module connectors — is designed to be replaced. We stock spare parts indefinitely for every product we've ever made.

🧩

Modular Reduces Waste

The traditional model: own three bags, replace each every few years. The TrailVolt model: own one base pack for decades, swap modules as your life changes. Our data shows modular buyers replace their main pack 73% less often than single-purpose bag buyers.

📋

Materials Transparency

We publish a full materials breakdown for every product we make. No vague "premium nylon" claims. Denier count, weave type, coating specification, hardware supplier — it's all disclosed because you deserve to know exactly what you're buying.

🏔️

Built for a Decade

We design every TrailVolt pack to outlast fashion cycles, trend cycles, and tech cycles. Our five-year warranty and lifetime repair programme aren't marketing — they're the natural consequence of building something we're actually proud of.

🌍

No Greenwashing

We won't claim carbon neutrality we haven't verified. We won't use "eco" as a marketing word without substance. Our sustainability page will always tell you exactly what we do — and where we're still working to improve.

Design for Longevity

Why We Build It to Last

Most gear brands design for three to five years of use — long enough to feel premium, short enough to ensure repeat purchases. We made a deliberate choice not to do this.

A TrailVolt Core Pack should last fifteen years under normal use. The modularity means you extend its life instead of replacing it — adding new modules as they're released, repairing individual components, passing it on when you're done.

We're building a customer relationship that lasts decades, not seasons. The best way to achieve that is to make something so good it never needs to be replaced.

Where We're Still Working

We believe in honesty. These are the areas where we're making progress — but haven't yet solved.

Carbon footprint tracking

In Progress

We're currently working with a third-party sustainability consultancy to calculate and publish the full cradle-to-gate carbon footprint of each product. We expect to publish our first Carbon Report in Q4 2026.

Recycled shell fabrics

Prototyping

We're testing rPET shell materials across three prototype rounds. The performance hasn't yet matched virgin nylon in abrasion resistance. We won't ship a product that underperforms — but we're close, and we will get there.

End-of-life take-back programme

Planned 2027

We're designing a take-back programme for packs at true end of life (15+ years, beyond repair). We plan to partner with textile recyclers to close the loop completely. This is scheduled for launch in 2027.
